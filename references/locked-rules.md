# Locked rules

Enforce before and after generation.

1. Use only 16:9.
2. Keep white/light gray/near-black dominant; control color.
3. Advance chapter gradients continuously through the spectrum.
4. Render chapter pages as full-screen gradients with oversized white titles.
5. Left-align ordinary titles.
6. Use one primary visual focal point per slide.
7. Let mesh visuals enter only from an edge and never cover content.
8. Use dark regions only for data, comparison, emphasis, or closing.
9. Use gray chart tracks with controlled gradient data colors.
10. Make key numbers at least 2.5x the size of explanatory type.
11. Do not use heavy shadows, glassmorphism, or full-page UI card grids.
12. Keep icons in one system; never use emoji.
13. Do not copy Google logos, trademarks, branded footer copy, or mesh artwork.
14. Do not alter input facts, numbers, units, qualifiers, or sources.
15. Allow no overflow, unintended overlap, broken glyphs, split words, or stretched images.
16. Do not repeat the same template silhouette for three consecutive pages.
17. Do not place three high-density data pages consecutively.
18. Insert at least one low-density breathing page every 3-5 pages.
19. Keep dark/full-or-half-dark pages between 20% and 35% for decks of six or more slides.
20. Give each substantive chapter a divider, an evidence page, and a synthesis/closing page.
21. Never construct a chapter gradient or decorative gradient from adjacent solid-color rectangles.
22. Use one continuous full-canvas gradient plus one or two soft radial diffusion fields on chapter pages.
23. On ordinary light pages, keep saturated color atmospheric and edge-bound; do not split the canvas into large red/yellow/green/blue blocks.
24. Reserve hard color blocks for compact labels, table headers, chart marks, or a deliberate dark evidence panel.
25. Reject any rendered page whose primary color transition has a visible rectangular seam.
26. Use emotional editorial imagery only when the slide's communication job is narrative, cultural, aspirational, or transitional.
27. Require one coherent tonal treatment and one crop vocabulary for every multi-image emotional composition.
28. Do not use ethnicity, nationality, or generic foreignness as a substitute for image intent.
29. Keep all overlaid text editable and limit image-window text to one to three words.
30. Never copy photographs, wordmarks, or proprietary brand shapes from a visual reference.
31. Select and record `visual_only`, `full_rebuild`, or `new_deck` before planning.
32. In `visual_only`, preserve slide count, order, visible audience copy, facts, and communication jobs.
33. Never expose TODOs, image prompts, editor instructions, comments, or production notes to the audience.
34. Use original project images before sourcing or generating replacements.
35. Keep audience text editable; use OOXML z-order repair before considering raster fallbacks.
36. Treat automated rhythm and style heuristics as advisory when their remediation would violate the selected mode.
