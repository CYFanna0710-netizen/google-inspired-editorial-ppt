# Image strategy

## Image roles

Assign every visual one role:

- `evidence`: proves a project, result, option, or process;
- `context`: establishes the environment or use case;
- `process`: shows collaboration, workflow, or decision-making;
- `material`: shows texture, finish, lighting, product, or physical detail;
- `atmosphere`: sets emotion on a low-density narrative page;
- `decorative`: supports rhythm without carrying meaning; use sparingly.

## Preferred visual grammar

- one dominant image with one supporting detail;
- before/after or day/night pairs;
- panoramic architectural strip;
- asymmetric process scene plus material detail;
- three to five related frames showing a controlled progression;
- full-bleed cover or closing image with a deliberate negative-space field.

Avoid equal photo-card grids unless the images are genuinely peer evidence.

## Editorial treatment

- Use consistent crop logic within a sequence.
- Match black level, warmth, grain, and contrast.
- Preserve faces, hands, screens, products, and architectural focal lines.
- Prefer restrained, believable photography over glossy stock brightness.
- Use warm editorial imagery with amber/copper accents; use spectral imagery with cool neutrals and controlled chromatic diffusion.

## Image-generation prompt record

Record subject, action, environment, lighting, camera distance, crop, negative space, palette, realism level, aspect ratio, and prohibited elements. Generate without text, logos, watermarks, decorative UI, or impossible lighting unless requested.

## Reuse rule

Do not repeat a project image across unrelated slides. A background may recur only as an intentional narrative bookend.
